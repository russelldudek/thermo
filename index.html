Enterprise AI and data architecture

Make AI architecture load-bearing.

At Thermo Fisher, AI should scale like a scientific platform: modular at the edge, governed at the core, and instrumented end to end. The architect's job is to make that architecture reusable enough to accelerate teams and rigorous enough to earn scientific trust.

The company moment

A highly diverse scientific enterprise is accelerating AI.

Thermo Fisher's digital ecosystem spans instruments, lab automation, data platforms, informatics, clinical research, manufacturing, and commercial systems. The architecture mandate is not a single platform decision. It is the discipline that keeps distributed innovation coherent.

The real mandate

Standardize the load-bearing parts without flattening the science.

The role is the control point between distributed solution demand and enterprise-grade reuse. Squads should move faster because authority, security, reliability, observability, cost, integration, and lifecycle ownership are already designed into the path.

Architecture contract

Every reference pattern carries six explicit boundaries.

The purpose is not more review theater. It is to make reuse safe, exceptions visible, and delivery guidance concrete.

01

Outcome boundary

Workflow, decision, owner, baseline, consequence, and measurable result.

02

Authority boundary

Authoritative context, model inference, human decision, abstention, and escalation.

03

Control boundary

Privacy, security, compliance, lineage, retention, evaluation, and audit.

04

Integration boundary

API, event, microservice, ERP, CRM, RPA, and data-platform contracts.

05

Operating boundary

Observability, SLOs, drift, incidents, rollback, cost, and capacity.

06

Reuse boundary

Ownership, versioning, documentation, adoption support, and promotion evidence.

Balance outcome, platform health, trust, economics, adoption, and learning so one dimension does not quietly transfer risk elsewhere.

The hard objection
“He has not held the formal enterprise AI architect title or owned our exact stack.”

That is a real gap. The response is evidence, not title inflation.

Russell does not claim production ownership of Thermo Fisher's exact AWS/Azure, Databricks, Dataiku, vector-database, or UiPath estate. The transferability case is high-reliability technical systems, architecture-like decision mechanisms, cross-platform workflow integration, AI and retrieval patterns, governance, human authority, observability, cost discipline, and adoption. The first proof should be one bounded reference pattern reviewed with existing architects and domain experts.
